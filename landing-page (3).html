The Paradox

The more you chase, the faster she pulls away.

You text first. You reply fast. You try harder to keep her interested — and somehow, she gets colder. Meanwhile there's that one guy who does almost nothing, and people gravitate toward him anyway. It looks unfair. It looks like he was born that way.

He wasn't. He just stopped auditioning. Attention, jokes, constant availability all communicate the same thing when overused: I need something from you. The moment she senses that need, the dynamic flips.

"You're not here to audition. You're here to see who earns your attention."

This guide breaks that pattern down and rebuilds it — not as a trick or a manipulation, but as an internal shift in how you carry yourself around people you're interested in.
